who I am

Future Space Research Engineer Passionate about space exploration and advanced technologies, I am a 5th-year student at IPSA, specializing in aerospace systems applied to the space sector. To further strengthen my expertise, I am pursuing a dual degree in Newspace technologies, where I combine technical excellence with a deep understanding of the future challenges in the space industry.

My Academic and Professional Journey

From an early stage, I have been driven by a curiosity to understand and develop the systems that make space exploration possible. My academic journey at IPSA has provided me with a solid foundation in aerospace engineering, systems design, and robotics. In parallel, I have embraced opportunities to work on ambitious projects and gain hands-on experience:

  • Internship at the LATMOS, Haute-Provence Observatory (OHP): I contributed to projects involving LIDAR technology for atmospheric and space research, which strengthened my analytical and technical skills in space observation systems.
  • Airbus Internship,Assistant Companion: I worked on aircraft manufacturing processes, where I learned the importance of precision, quality, and teamwork in large-scale aerospace projects.
  • Manager Assistant at Broetje Automation: This experience allowed me to develop strong project management and problem-solving skills while working on automated systems for aircraft assembly.
  • Orly Industrie Internship, Aircraft Engine Maintenance: I gained practical experience in engine diagnostics, troubleshooting, and repair, reinforcing my understanding of complex mechanical systems

My Skills and Expertise

Throughout my journey, I have developed a strong technical and analytical skillset, which includes:

  • Systems Engineering: Designing and integrating aerospace systems, with a focus on space missions and satellite development.
  • Robotics and Programming: Developing autonomous systems using languages such as C++, particularly in complex navigation and control applications.
  • Space Systems Design: Participating in the conception of CubeSats and satellite systems for missions such as SIMPLEx, involving deep-space exploration and planetary observation.
  • Project Management: Leading and contributing to projects with a focus on technical precision, teamwork, and meeting critical deadlines.

My Ambition

My goal is clear: to become a space research engineer. I aspire to contribute to the design and development of innovative technologies that will push the boundaries of space exploration, whether it involves planetary observation, robotics for space missions, or advanced satellite systems.
Curious, rigorous, and always eager to learn, I strive to combine my technical expertise with a strong vision for the future of space innovation.

Scouting has taught me essential values such as teamwork, leadership, and perseverance, which I carry into every project I undertake.
